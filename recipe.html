Yamarita

Yamarita, known as Dundun Oniyeriis a meal you make by frying flour coated boiled Yam. Like fries, it can be eaten alone but it is best enjoyed with its special savoury sauce. It is a meal local to the Yoruba speaking people of Nigeria so if you have been longing to try out a meal from West Africa that is not rice based, you have come to the right place. Let's start cooking!!!

Ingredients

  • 1/2 Tuber of medium Yam cut into rectangular pieces

  • 1 Egg

  • 1 Cup Plain Flour

  • Cooking Oil

  • 1 Teaspoon Cayenne pepper

  • 1 Bouillon cube

  • 1/2 Teaspoon Curry Powder

  • 1 Teaspoon Aromat Seasoning

  • 1 Teaspoon Garlic Powder (Optional)

  • Salt to taste

Directions

  • Slice and peel the skin off the yam.Wash the yam thoroughly, place in a small pot add water to cover the yam, add just a little bit of salt and boil on medium heat for 10 minutes
  • Boil till its tender but still very firm, remember you're still going to fry the yam to complete the cooking process. Drain the water afterwards and leave to cool.
  • On a flat plate, combine the flour,pepper, bouillon cube, curry powder and other spices and set aside.Then break the egg into a flat plate, add salt to taste and set aside, you can also add a bit of seasoning.
  • Dip each yam into the egg mixture(helps in the next step where you will be coating the yam with the flour mixture.Also enhances the crunchiness of the yam.
  • When that's done, place each yam into the flour mixture, coat the yam chunks with the flour mixture ensuring each yam is well covered. When you're done coating the yam, set them on a flat plate.
  • Again dip each flour coated yam into the egg mixture, ensure the egg mixture covers the yam as much as possible
  • Now add the cooking oil into a hollow pan, set it on low-medium heat, when the oil heats up, dip each flour and egg coated yam into the oil and fry on all sides till slightly brown.
